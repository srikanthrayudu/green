Green Building

A green building is an eco-friendly and sustainable structure designed to minimize its environmental impact. It incorporates features like energy and water efficiency, the use of sustainable materials, waste reduction, and considerations for indoor air quality. Green buildings aim to promote resource conservation, reduce greenhouse gas emissions, and enhance overall environmental sustainability.

LEED (Leadership in Energy and Environmental Design) certification is a widely recognized standard for assessing and promoting the sustainability of buildings based on various environmental performance criteria.

BREEAM (Building Research Establishment Environmental Assessment Method) is a widely used green building certification system that assesses and rates the environmental performance of buildings based on various sustainability criteria.

ENERGY STAR certification is a government-backed designation awarded to energy-efficient products, buildings, and industrial facilities that meet strict energy performance criteria.

IGBC certification, offered by the Indian Green Building Council, recognizes buildings that meet environmentally sustainable and energy-efficient standards.
